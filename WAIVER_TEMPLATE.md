## Purpose

This waiver template is designed for use by the Motorcycle Off-Road Explorers (MORE) Club for organized rides and events. It should be signed by all participants before each event or at the beginning of each riding season.

## Key Components

A proper liability waiver should include:

1. **Assumption of Risk** - Participant acknowledges the dangers
2. **Release of Liability** - Participant agrees not to sue
3. **Indemnification** - Participant agrees to cover costs if they do sue
4. **Medical Authorization** - Permission for emergency medical treatment
5. **Photo/Media Release** - Optional, for publicity use
6. **Acknowledgment of Understanding** - Confirms they read and understood

## ACKNOWLEDGMENT OF RISKS

I acknowledge and understand that participating in off-road motorcycle riding and related activities ("the Activity") involves inherent risks, dangers, and hazards, including but not limited to:

### Riding Risks
- Loss of control of motorcycle
- Collision with other riders, vehicles, or objects
- Collision with terrain features (rocks, trees, obstacles)
- Mechanical failure of motorcycle
- Unpredictable actions of other riders
- Varying and hazardous terrain conditions
- Inadequate or faulty equipment

### Environmental Risks
- Extreme weather conditions (heat, cold, rain, wind)
- Limited visibility conditions
- Wildlife encounters
- Remote locations with limited access to emergency services
- Trail conditions (mud, dust, water crossings, steep grades)
- Falling rocks, branches, or other natural hazards

### Physical Risks
- Serious personal injury including death
- Fractures, sprains, and broken bones
- Head, neck, and spinal injuries
- Internal injuries
- Lacerations and abrasions
- Concussion and traumatic brain injury
- Permanent disability
- Mental or emotional trauma

### Other Risks
- Inadequate first aid or emergency response
- Errors in judgment by ride leaders or other participants
- Negligent first aid or rescue operations
- The unavailability of immediate medical attention

I understand that these risks may be caused by my own actions or inactions, the actions or inactions of others participating in the Activity, the condition of the premises or equipment, or THE NEGLIGENCE of the Released Parties (as defined below).

I voluntarily assume all risks associated with participation in this Activity, whether or not described above, EVEN IF ARISING FROM THE NEGLIGENCE of the Released Parties.

## RELEASE OF LIABILITY

In consideration of being permitted to participate in the Activity, I, for myself and on behalf of my heirs, assigns, personal representatives, and next of kin, HEREBY RELEASE, INDEMNIFY, AND HOLD HARMLESS the Motorcycle Off-Road Explorers (MORE) Club and its officers, officials, agents, employees, volunteers, members, ride leaders, event organizers, landowners, and sponsors (collectively, the "Released Parties") WITH RESPECT TO ANY AND ALL INJURY, DISABILITY, DEATH, or loss or damage to person or property, WHETHER ARISING FROM THE NEGLIGENCE of the Released Parties or otherwise, to the fullest extent permitted by law.

---

## INDEMNIFICATION

I AGREE TO INDEMNIFY AND HOLD HARMLESS the Released Parties from any loss, liability, damage, or cost they may incur arising out of or related to my participation in the Activity, WHETHER CAUSED BY THE NEGLIGENCE of the Released Parties or otherwise, to the fullest extent permitted by law.

I agree to reimburse the Released Parties for any costs, including attorney fees, incurred in defending against any claims I might bring or that might be brought on my behalf.

---

## MEDICAL AUTHORIZATION

I authorize the Released Parties and their agents to obtain or provide emergency medical care for me if needed. I understand and agree that:

1. I am solely responsible for all costs associated with medical care and transportation
2. The Released Parties assume no responsibility for any medical treatment provided or not provided
3. I will not hold the Released Parties liable for any claims arising from emergency medical treatment
4. I have adequate health insurance to cover any medical expenses I may incur

**Medical Consent for Minors:** If the participant is under 18 years of age, the parent/guardian signing below authorizes emergency medical treatment for the minor participant.

## EQUIPMENT AND SAFETY ACKNOWLEDGMENTS

I acknowledge and agree that:

1. **My Motorcycle:** I certify that my motorcycle is in safe operating condition and properly equipped for off-road riding
2. **Safety Gear:** I will wear appropriate safety equipment including DOT/Snell approved helmet, eye protection, gloves, boots, and protective clothing
3. **Skills:** I possess the necessary skills and physical fitness for the planned Activity
4. **Rules:** I will follow all applicable laws, regulations, and instructions from ride leaders
5. **Responsibility:** I am solely responsible for my own safety and the safety of others I may affect
6. **Right to Refuse:** Ride leaders have the right to exclude me from participation if they determine I am unprepared or unsafe

## IMPORTANT NOTES FOR CLUB ORGANIZERS

### Before Using This Waiver:

1. **Legal Review:** Have an attorney licensed in your state review and customize this waiver
2. **State Requirements:** Research your state's specific requirements for liability waivers
3. **Insurance:** Maintain appropriate liability insurance regardless of waiver
4. **Minors:** Some states do not allow parents to waive a minor's rights
5. **Updates:** Review and update waiver annually with legal counsel
6. **Enforcement:** Ensure all participants sign before participating
7. **Records:** Maintain signed waivers for minimum of 7 years (or as advised by attorney)

### Red Flags That Require Legal Review:

- Operating across state lines
- Commercial operations or paid events
- Participants from out of state
- Minors participating
- High-risk activities beyond typical trail riding
- Use of club-owned equipment
- Professional instruction or training
- Land use agreements or special permits

### Best Practices:

1. **Legibility:** Ensure waiver is readable and not in tiny print
2. **Understanding:** Allow participants time to read before signing
3. **Questions:** Answer any questions about the waiver
4. **Copies:** Provide participants with a copy of the signed waiver
5. **Annual:** Consider having participants sign annually, not just once
6. **Per Event:** For high-risk events, consider event-specific waivers
7. **Documentation:** Keep waivers organized and easily accessible
