# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

The Motorcycle Off-Road Explorers (MORE) Club is committed to providing a welcoming, safe, and inclusive environment for all members and participants, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to make participation in our club and community a harassment-free experience for everyone, both online and on the trails.

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

We expect all community members, including riders, contributors, and visitors, to:

**On the Trails:**
- Practice safe riding and follow all safety guidelines
- Respect the skill levels and comfort zones of all riders
- Support fellow riders, especially those in need of assistance
- Treat the environment with respect (Leave No Trace principles)
- Follow all applicable laws, regulations, and land use policies
- Respect private property and other trail users
- Maintain proper equipment and preparedness standards

**In Community Interactions:**
- Use welcoming and inclusive language
- Be respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Accept constructive feedback gracefully
- Focus on what's best for the community
- Show empathy towards other community members
- Share knowledge and mentor new riders

**Safety First:**
- Never pressure anyone to attempt terrain beyond their abilities
- Speak up about safety concerns without fear of judgment
- Follow the "no rider left behind" principle
- Report safety incidents or near-misses to help others learn
- Respect the decision when someone says "I'm not comfortable with this"

### Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are considered unacceptable:

**Harassment and Discrimination:**
- Offensive comments related to gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, age, religion, or nationality
- Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following (online or in-person)
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Sustained disruption of rides, events, or online discussions
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission

**Safety Violations:**
- Riding under the influence of alcohol or drugs
- Reckless riding that endangers others
- Deliberately leaving a rider behind or isolated
- Ignoring or dismissing safety concerns
- Pressuring others to ride beyond their skill level or comfort zone
- Operating unsafe or improperly maintained equipment after being informed of issues

**Environmental Violations:**
- Riding off designated trails without authorization
- Damaging vegetation, wildlife habitat, or archaeological sites
- Littering or improper waste disposal
- Ignoring fire restrictions or other environmental regulations
- Disturbing or harassing wildlife

**Community Violations:**
- Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments
- Personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Advocating for or encouraging any of the above behaviors

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all MORE Club spaces, including:

- Club rides and events
- Online spaces (Discord, GitHub, email, social media)
- Club meetings and gatherings
- One-on-one communications related to club business
- Representing the club in public forums

This Code of Conduct also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces.

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any other concerns, please report it as soon as possible:

**Email:** more@ride-more.org (with subject line "Code of Conduct Issue")

**What to Include:**
- Your contact information
- Names (or descriptions) of individuals involved
- Description of the behavior and circumstances
- Date, time, and location
- Any additional context or witnesses
- Whether the situation is ongoing

All reports will be handled with discretion. We will protect the identity of reporters to the extent possible while conducting a fair investigation.

### Investigation Process

1. **Acknowledgment:** Reports will be acknowledged within 48 hours
2. **Review:** The situation will be reviewed by club leadership
3. **Investigation:** Relevant parties may be contacted for information
4. **Decision:** Appropriate action will be determined based on the severity and context
5. **Communication:** Involved parties will be informed of the outcome
6. **Documentation:** Incidents will be documented for pattern recognition

### Consequences

Depending on the severity and circumstances, consequences may include:

**First Violations (Minor):**
- Private written warning
- Mediation between parties
- Temporary exclusion from specific activities

**Serious or Repeated Violations:**
- Temporary suspension from club activities (30-90 days)
- Removal from online community spaces
- Exclusion from club leadership or event organizing roles

**Severe Violations:**
- Permanent ban from all club activities and spaces
- Notification to relevant authorities (for illegal activities)
- Public statement about the situation (if necessary for community safety)

**Immediate Actions:**
- In cases of imminent danger or ongoing harassment, individuals may be immediately removed from an event or online space pending investigation
- Ride leaders have authority to exclude anyone from a ride for safety violations

## Special Considerations for Outdoor Activities

### Risk Acknowledgment

Off-road motorcycle riding carries inherent risks. This Code of Conduct does not eliminate those risks or create liability for accidents that occur during normal riding activities.

### Emergency Situations

In emergency situations (injury, lost rider, severe weather, equipment failure), safety takes absolute priority over all other considerations. Actions taken in good faith during genuine emergencies will be evaluated with this context in mind.

### Skill Level Honesty

Riders are expected to honestly assess and communicate their skill level. Ride leaders may make judgments about appropriate participation based on observed skills and terrain requirements.

## Appeals Process

Individuals who have been sanctioned under this Code of Conduct may appeal the decision:

1. Submit a written appeal to more@ride-more.org within 14 days
2. Clearly state why you believe the decision was incorrect
3. Provide any additional context or evidence
4. Appeals will be reviewed by club leadership not involved in the original decision
5. Appeal decisions are final

## Amendments

This Code of Conduct may be amended by club leadership as the organization evolves. Community input is welcomed. Current version will always be available at [ride-more.org](https://ride-more.org).

## Questions

If you have questions about this Code of Conduct or need clarification on any point, please contact us at more@ride-more.org.
